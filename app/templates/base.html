foodloggr

Simple Food Logging.

Traditional food logging apps require more effort than they really should. You have to search for food items in an enormous database, where official entries are mixed in with user-submitted entries, and a lot of the time those databases are very US-centric. This makes it difficult to track what you are eating, often leading to just giving up on using the service. foodloggr aims to simplify that process – you just enter what you eat, that’s it.

foodloggr is built using the Flask framework for Python, and is hosted on Amazon EC2 with user content stored on S3.

foodloggr was covered by //Startup News in October 2015. You can read the article here.

Try foodloggr today!

qrByte

Multipurpose QR codes.

qrByte is a simple web application that allows users to create intelligent QR codes. The QR codes are capable of detecting the type of mobile device being used for scanning, and redirecting the user to the appropriate URL based on their device type. Perfect for QR codes linking to apps on the iTunes and Google Play stores!

Try qrByte today!

Simple Invoicing

No frills invoicing for freelancers.

I created Simple Invoicing because I was sick of manually creating invoices for clients in Microsoft Word. Simple Invoicing allows you to add the basic details of your clients and create invoices for those clients. It also has a dashboard, advising you of outstanding and overdue invoices.
